# How to use this guide

## Who this is for

You are someone who understands the basics of machine learning, deep learning, and AI. You know **zero neuroscience**. You want to understand the brain well enough to:
- read neuro-inspired AI papers (predictive coding, world models, Hopfield nets, SNNs) without bluffing,
- form your own view on what AGI/ASI is missing,
- have informed conversations with computational neuroscientists.

This is **not** a neuroscience textbook. It is a high-velocity bridge from Computer science into the parts of neuro that matter for AI research. This guide is a curated list of articles with links to relevant research papers without a paywall.

## Conventions

- **🤖 AI-relevance** boxes call out where each idea lands in modern ML.
- **📄 Papers** are linked inline, with one-line summaries; full annotated list in Ch 27.
- **🔗 External-first.** Most reading happens on the public web (textbook sites, arXiv, Neuromatch, Scholarpedia). This local server gives you the spine and the commentary.
- **🧪 Try-it** boxes suggest a small experiment a CS person can run in a notebook.

## What you should be able to do after

1. Read a Nature Neuroscience paper and extract the computational claim.
2. Critique a "neuro-inspired" AI paper for whether it's actually neuro-inspired.
3. Map an LLM behavior to a candidate neural mechanism (and know when no such mapping exists).
4. Pitch a research direction at the neuro × AI boundary that isn't already crowded.
